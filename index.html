1.0 SUMMARY‌‌

This report summarizes results of the validation of M_MET_QC_0090: Measurement of RTP004 Peptide Antibodies in Human Serum By Antigen Capture ELISA (draft). Results for the following validation characteristics: Assay Cut-Point, Specificity, Linearity, Low Limit of Detection (LLOD), and Low Limit of Quantitation (LLOQ), Accuracy, Precision (Repeatability, Intermediate Precision), Robustness (Min/Max Incubation Tolerance), and Sample Stability are presented in this report.

This validation was performed per validation protocol of PROT_VAL_0884, Peptide Antibodies Validation Protocol for M_MET_QC_0090: Measurement of RTP004 Peptide Antibodies in Human Serum by Antigen Capture ELISA, by Revance Quality Control Unit.


2.0 BACKGROUND

Many biotechnology-derived pharmaceuticals are intended to induce an unwanted immune response, which may lead to allergic reactions, altered pharmacokinetics and reduced efficacy.

A screening ELISA method to measure anti-peptide (RTP004) antibodies was validated by AI Biotech (SOP IMMUNO00013). to support clinical studies at Revance Due to technical issues, method transfer from AI Biotech to Revance was not successful. Attempts to transfer the method internally had generated incomparable results from AI Biotech’s reported data. Further efforts were made to determine the causes of high background signal encountered when using the method and various optimization steps were conducted to re-develop the method for measuring anti- peptide antibodies in human serum samples at Revance (see development report RT001-DR109R, RTP004 Peptide Antibodies in Human Serum by Antigen Capture ELISA).

Based on the development study, the method of Measurement of RTP004 Peptide Antibodies in Human Serum By Antigen Capture ELISA (M_MET_QC_0090) is drafted. In the assay, peptide (RTP004) immobilized on a Maxisorp ELISA plate is used to capture antibodies recognizing RTP004 in human serum samples/ rabbit anti-peptide positive control samples, the bound antibodies are detected with biotinylated polyclonal anti-human IgG or anti-rabbit IgG (positive control in the assay) followed by horseradish peroxidase (HRP) and visualized with 3,3’,5,5’-

tetramethylbenzidine (TMB) substrate. The signal is measured for the absorbance at 450 nm.

In this study, QC method M_MET_QC_0090 was validated for its intended use to detect anti-peptide (RTP004) antibodies in human serum samples obtained from individuals dosed with RTT150 DP (contains RTP004).

Results presented in Table 1 below demonstrated that the acceptance criteria as described in the validation protocol were met.

5.0 PROCEDURE


    1. Assay Cut-Point

      Assay cut-point value is used to determine whether a sample is positive or negative in the assay, a sample is considered as positive for anti-peptide antibodies when the sample gives an absorbance reading greater than the assay cut-point value.

      The assay cut-point value was determined in this validation by evaluating the variability in the assay of normal human serum samples from a panel of 100 untreated normal human individuals (Bioreclamation, Westbury, NY). Each individual sample was diluted into 5% fetal bovine serum (FBS)/1X PBS at dilution of 1:100 and assayed in duplicate by two analysts. OD values of the 100 samples were used to calculate the mean and standard deviation. The

      t-value corresponding to the 95th percentile (one tailed) was multiplied by the overall standard deviation and added to the mean value of the 100 normal human serum samples to calculate the overall assay cut-point value, which was translated into the following formula:


      Assay cut-point value = Overall assay mean + (t-value)(SD)


      One percent of pooled normal human serum was prepared in 5% FBS/ 1xPBS and analyzed in 8 replicates as the negative control for each assay. OD readings of the 1% pooled normal human serum sample (ODNeg Ctl) from all the cut-point screening assays were averaged to give the overall mean

      ODNeg Ctl value.


      Cut-point factor (X) was determined as the difference between the overall assay cut-point value and the overall mean OD reading from the pooled normal human serum negative control sample (ODNeg Ctl) as follows:


      Cut point factor = Overall assay cut point value – Overall mean ODNeg Ctl

      For future assays, this cut-point factor is added to the mean absorbance value of the 1% pooled human serum negative control to determine assay cut-point for each particular assay.


    2. Specificity‌‌‌

Specificity was evaluated by cross test of anti-peptide antibody positive control samples with both peptide and toxin coated plates. A rabbit

anti-peptide polyclonal antibody prepared at 30 ng/mL in 1% pooled normal human serum, 5% FBS/ 1xPBS was used as the anti-peptide positive control and 1% pooled normal human serum in 5% FBS/ 1xPBS was used as the negative control. The 30 ng/mL (not the 60 ng/mL listed in the validation protocol) of rabbit anti-peptide polyclonal antibody was prepared as the positive control during this validation because the 60 ng/mL antibody OD reading was too high.

The positive and negative control samples were tested on both peptide (RTP004) coated plate and toxin (RTT150) coated plate.


5.3 Linearity, Low Limit of Detection (LLOD), and Low Limit of Quantification (LLOQ)


Linearity describes variables that change proportionally and can graphically be represented as a straight line. A set of serial diluted rabbit anti-peptide polyclonal antibody samples were prepared to evaluate the OD reading response corresponded to the concentration changes of the spiked rabbit

anti-peptide antibody and tested in 8 replicates for each concentration for the linearity assay. The rabbit anti-peptide antibody was prepared in 1% pooled human serum to simulator the clinical sample test condition (1:100 dilution in the assay). 5% FBS/1xPBS in the preparation was used to block high nonspecific binding from the human serum. The linearity was prepared as shown in the following Table 2, which was deviated from the validation protocol because the high OD reading for the 240 ng/mL sample.     1. Accuracy‌‌

      Rabbit anti-peptide polyclonal antibody was used for preparation of the accuracy samples. The accuracy samples were prepared at 3 different concentrations by spiking a known amount of the antibody into the assay matrix, 1% pooled normal human serum/ 5% FBS/ 1xPBS, to achieve the antibody concentration of 40 ng/mL, 30 ng/mL and 20 ng/mL respectively. The accuracy samples were prepared at 40 ng/mL, 30 ng/mL and 20 ng/mL instead of the 70 ng/mL, 60 ng/mL and 50 ng/mL as indicated in the validation protocol (see Deviation Section 8.0)

      Three individually prepared accuracy samples were tested for each concentration in 5 replicates by two analysts. Accuracy samples were tested along with a standard calibration curve for the recovery analysis.


    2. Precision – Repeatability and Intermediate Precision

      OD reading values of the 8 replicates from the linearity assays of the rabbit anti-peptide antibody samples with 7 different concentrations were analyzed for the inter assay precision.

      Intermediate precision was determined by analyzing OD readings from the middle concentration accuracy sample tested by two analysts of 6 sets of data (3 samples x 2 analysts).


    3. Robustness

      Robustness was assessed by examining the minimum and maximum tolerance at different incubation steps to show that it has no effect on the performance of the assay or the quality of the data generated. The tolerance of each significant incubation step was evaluated in the assay as follows:


      • Plate Coating: 16 hours–72 hours


      • Plate Blocking: 1 hour–3 hours


      • Sample Incubation: 55 minutes–65 minutes


      • Biotin Conjugate Incubation: 55 minutes–65 minutes


      • HRP Conjugate Incubation: 25 minutes–35 minutes


      • TMB Substrate Incubation: 10 minutes–15 minutes

        Two plates were prepared for testing each of the above incubation tolerance steps, one plate tested the minimum tolerance conditions and the other one plate tested the maximum tolerance conditions. A positive control was prepared using the rabbit anti-peptide antibody and tested on both plates to evaluate the OD response of the positive control under the minimum and maximum tolerance conditions.

        A negative control (1% Pooled normal human serum in 5% FBS /1xPBS) was also tested under both minimum and maximum tolerance conditions.


    4. Sample Stability‌‌

The rabbit anti-peptide polyclonal antibody was used to prepare the stability samples. Stability samples were prepared at 30 ng/mL in 1% pooled normal human serum/ 5%FBS/ 1xPBS. These samples were subjected to 1, 2 and 3 Freeze/Thaw cycles respectively, and one sample from each stress condition was stored at 2 ºC–8 ºC or room temperature for 6 hours.

The purpose of this validation step was to examine the sample (using rabbit anti-peptide antibody to mimic the anti-peptide antibody from clinical study human samples) stability under each of the stress condition and to examine sample stability when in-use.


6.0 RESULTS


    1. Assay Cut-Point

      100 human serum samples from untreated normal human individuals (Bioreclamation, Westbury, NY) were assayed in duplicate by two analysts to generate two set of data.

      The OD values of the 100 samples from the two set assays were used to calculate the overall mean and standard deviation. The t-value corresponding to the 95th percentile (one tailed) was multiplied by the overall standard deviation and added to the mean value of the 100 normal human serum samples to calculate the overall assay cut-point value, which was translated into the following formula:

      Assay cut-point value = Overall assay mean + (t-value)(SD) Negative control sample (1% pooled normal human serum in

      5% FBS/ 1xPBS) was tested on each assay plate. The average OD of the negative control (Mean ODNeg Ctl) from all plates in the cut-point screening assays was used to calculate the assay cut point factor (X).

      The cut point factor (X) determined from this validation is used to calculate the future assay cut point value, this factor (X) will be added to the mean of the assay negative control to determine the assay cut point value for each particular assay.

      Positive control sample prepared with the rabbit anti-peptide antibody was used to assess the assay acceptance criteria. OD readings of normal human individual serum samples on each assay plate were averaged (assay mean). Assay cut point value for each assay plate was calculated by adding

      1.7SD (t*SD) to the assay mean to determine assay acceptance criteria for each assay.

    2. Specificity

The purpose of specificity test was to show that in this ELISA assay the anti-peptide antibody bind specifically to its antigen (RTP004). Positive control samples were tested on both peptide coated (specific binding) and toxin coated (non-specific) plates. OD value of positive control was much

higher than the negative control sample on the peptide coated plate, while no difference was seen between the positive control sample and the negative control sample on the toxin coated plate. These results confirmed that the


positive OD reading response from the positive control (rabbit anti-peptide antibody) was resulted from the sample specific binding to the peptide in the ELISA assay. The specificity of the ELISA assay was further confirmed by the Linearity test, which showed the anti-peptide antibody bound in a‌‌

dose-dependent manner to the peptide immobilized on the ELISA plates

(Figure 1).

Assay cut point value was established using the mean OD of negative control from each plate during the specificity tests plus the cut point factor (calculated from the cut point value assays).

The following acceptance criteria were met for the Specificity tests: the mean OD value of the positive control from the peptide coated plate > the assay

cut-point value and OD value of the positive control from the toxin coated plate < the assay cut-point value.

    4. Sample Stability

Sample stability was evaluated by testing samples with known amounts of rabbit anti-peptide antibody (30 ng/mL) spiked in 1% pooled normal human serum/ 5%FBS/ 1xPBS subjected to 1, 2 and 3 Freeze/Thaw cycles respectively, and one sample from each stress condition was stored at

2 ºC–8 ºC or room temperature for 6 hours.

A freshly prepared rabbit anti-peptide antibody (30 ng/mL) in 1% pooled normal human serum/ 5%FBS/ 1xPBS was used as the control. OD reading values of the stressed stability samples were compared to the OD values of the freshly prepared sample. OD values of the stressed samples were 114–120% of the freshly prepared sample, which met the acceptance criteria that the variation of OD values for the stressed samples must be within 30% of the fresh control.

    1. CONCLUSION

      Results from each of the validation characteristic reported in this study met all acceptance criteria set forth in the validation protocol.

      Method of M_MET_QC_0090 is validated to be used for measurement of

      anti-peptide (RTP004) antibodies in human serum with minimum 1:100 dilution by antigen capture ELISA.

      • Cut point factor is validated to be 0.459.

8.0 DEVIATION

The accuracy samples were prepared at 40 ng/mL, 30 ng/mL and 20 ng/mL instead of the 70 ng/mL, 60 ng/mL and 50 ng/mL as indicated in the validation protocol, because the high OD reading for the 50 ng/mL and higher concentration may fall outside of the calibration curve range (refer to development report RT001-DR109R, Amendment 1). These changes do not affect the quality of the validation.
